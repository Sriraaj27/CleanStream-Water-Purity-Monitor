Purity and quality measures of selected locations, along with their corresponding purification methods

Location: Hussain Sagar

Turbidity: High turbidity due to suspended solids

Color and Odor: Green in color with a Foul Smell

pH Levels: 7.8-8.2 (Neutral to slightly alkaline)

Chloride Concentrations: 270-400 mg/L > 250 mg/L

Total Dissolved Solids (TDS): 900-1200 mg/L > 500 mg/L

Purification Methods: Boiling, Homemade Sand & Charcoal Filters

Location: Gandipet Lake

Turbidity: Moderate-high due to organic matter

Color and Odor: Typically greenish with an earthy/musty smell

pH Levels: 7.5-8.5 (Slightly alkaline)

Chloride Concentrations: 50-150 mg/L < 250 mg/L

Total Dissolved Solids (TDS): 200-400 mg/L < 500 mg/L

Purification Methods: Plain Sedimentation, Cloth Filtration

Location: Durgam Cheruvu

Turbidity: Moderate to high (10-50 NTU) due to silt runoff

Color: Clearer on the northern and murky on the southern side

Odor: Earthy/musty and foul smell near polluted zones

pH Levels: Slightly alkaline (7.5-8.5) and spike (>9)

Chloride Concentrations: 100-300 mg/L, high near sewage inlets > 250 mg/L

Total Dissolved Solids (TDS): 500-1200 mg/L (varies by zone) > 500 mg/L

Purification Methods: Alum Clarification, Solar Disinfection (SODIS)
